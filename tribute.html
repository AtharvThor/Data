Dr. B. R. Ambedkar

Architect of the Indian Constitution

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar (1891–1956) was one of the greatest social reformers and thinkers in Indian history. Born into a Dalit family, he rose against all odds to become the chief architect of the Indian Constitution.

Ambedkar earned doctorates in law and economics from Columbia University and the London School of Economics. His life's work focused on fighting discrimination, ensuring social justice, and empowering marginalized communities.

As the chairman of the drafting committee of the Constitution of India, he ensured it guaranteed equality, liberty, and fraternity for all citizens. He also led movements to eradicate untouchability and promoted education as a tool of liberation.
